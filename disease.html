Insect Information

Aphids

  • Small, soft-bodied insects.
  • Typically green, black, or yellow.
  • Feed on plant sap.
  • Can transmit plant diseases.
  • Often found in colonies on plants.

Brown Stink Bug

  • Known for their unpleasant odor when disturbed.
  • Brown color with a shield-like shape.
  • Feed on a wide range of plants.
  • Can cause damage to crops.
  • Adult and nymph stages are visible.

Codling Moth

  • Common pest of apple and pear trees.
  • Larvae feed on fruit, causing damage.
  • Adults are small, with grayish-brown wings.
  • Can cause significant crop losses.
  • Control methods include traps and pesticides.

Caterpillar

  • Larval stage of butterflies and moths.
  • Usually feed on leaves and other plant material.
  • Can be pests in gardens and crops.
  • Come in various colors and patterns.
  • Transform into pupa (chrysalis) before becoming adults.

Leaf Roller

  • Larvae roll or fold leaves to form a protective shelter.
  • Can cause significant damage to crops.
  • Feed inside the rolled leaves.
  • Control methods include insecticides and natural predators.
  • Adults are small moths with narrow wings.

Ladybug

  • Small, brightly colored beetles.
  • Known for their red or orange bodies with black spots.
  • Feed on aphids and other small pests.
  • Beneficial for gardens and crops as they control pests.
  • Adults and larvae are both predatory.

Apple Diseases

Apple Scab

  • Fungal disease causing dark, sunken lesions on fruit and leaves.
  • Can cause defoliation and fruit drop.
  • Favored by cool, wet conditions.
  • Management includes fungicides and resistant varieties.
  • Prune infected plant parts and improve air circulation.

Fire Blight

  • Bacterial disease affecting blossoms, branches, and fruit.
  • Symptoms include blackened, wilting shoots and fruit.
  • Spreads rapidly in warm, wet weather.
  • Control includes pruning infected parts and applying antibiotics.
  • Resistant apple varieties can also be helpful.

Powdery Mildew

  • Fungal disease characterized by white, powdery spots on leaves and fruit.
  • Can lead to leaf distortion and reduced fruit quality.
  • Thrives in warm, dry conditions.
  • Control includes fungicide treatments and ensuring good air circulation.
  • Regularly remove affected plant parts.

Apple Rust

  • Fungal disease causing yellow or orange spots on leaves and fruit.
  • Can lead to premature leaf drop and reduced fruit yield.
  • Favored by cool, moist conditions.
  • Management includes removing infected plant debris and applying fungicides.
  • Consider resistant apple varieties.

Bitter Rot

  • Fungal disease causing circular, sunken lesions on fruit.
  • Infected areas can turn brown or black.
  • Occurs in hot and humid conditions.
  • Control by removing infected fruit and applying fungicides.
  • Improving air circulation and removing plant debris can help prevent outbreaks.

Crown Rot

  • Fungal disease that affects the roots and base of the tree.
  • Causes wilting and yellowing of leaves
